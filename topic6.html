Better
Every Day

I spent years eating whatever I wanted, moving as little as possible, and trusting that the system had my health figured out. I'm in my 50s now. The bill is coming due. Gut issues, back issues, a body that's been running on processed food, seed oils, and convenience for decades. Nobody did that to me. I did that to me.

So I'm changing it. Not overnight. Not perfectly. Just better than yesterday.

Here's what I'm learning: the things that actually help you aren't complicated and they aren't expensive. Morning sun on your face. Bare feet in wet grass. Breathing with intention. Cutting out the food that was never really food to begin with. The answers aren't in a prescription pad or a supplement commercial. They're mostly free, mostly ancient, and mostly ignored because there's no profit in telling you to go stand outside.

Fast food, seed oils, energy drinks, chemical-filled snacks — illegal in most other countries but perfectly approved here. Ask yourself why. Then put them down anyway.

Nobody's asking you to overhaul your life tomorrow. I'm not there yet either. But somewhere between where you are and where you want to be is one small change that makes everything else easier. Start there.

This is me figuring it out. Out loud. For the betterment of me.
